# AI Gateway Taste Layer

## What we optimize for

- **Clarity over decoration.** Every visual element must earn its place by
  making operational data faster to read.
- **Developer-tool density.** The dashboard is used for quick health checks,
  not marketing impressions. Information density is a feature.
- **Restrained color.** A dark canvas with one healthy accent (lime) and one
  warning accent (pink). Violet is structural, not expressive.

## Likes

- Dark-first technical surfaces (observability, terminals, IDEs).
- Uppercase tracked labels for panel headings and status chips.
- Monospace for model names, hashes, status codes, and any machine-facing
  value.
- Hairline borders and flat panels instead of shadows on dark backgrounds.
- Lime-on-violet for success states.
- Honest empty states: "No data yet" rather than optimistic placeholders.

## Dislikes

- Marketing-style heroes inside operational tools.
- Gradients used as decoration.
- Pill buttons unless they genuinely behave as toggles.
- More than two semantic accents on one screen.
- Shadows under dark cards.
- Decorative illustrations in data-dense views.

## Voice

Plain, direct, system-facing. Labels describe what is shown, not what the
product does. "Readiness", "Requests", "Recent Failures" — not "Your Gateway
Health" or "Usage Insights".

## Defaults to question

- Anything that makes the page taller without adding decision-relevant data.
- Any color that is not lime, pink, violet, or grayscale.
- Any animation beyond a single auto-refresh pulse.
